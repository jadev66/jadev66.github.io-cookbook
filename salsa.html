Salsa

Salsa is a party staple and a nutritious accent to your favorite dishes. There is no wrong way to make salsa and every ingredient is "to taste." Don't like jalapeño? Omit it. REALLY like jalapeño? Add to your heart's content. Here's a simple 15 minute version.

Ingredients

  • 10 vine ripe tomatoes
  • 3 bell peppers - 1 green, 1 yellow, 1 orange
  • 3 medium jalapeños
  • 1 medium white or yellow onion
  • 2 lemons
  • 4 limes
  • 1 bundle cilantro
  • 6 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by rough chopping the bell peppers, 4 tomatoes, and the onion. Add the chopped vegetables to the food processor and blitz until about diced-sized. You may need to scrape down the sides between blitzes.
  2. Now to address the jalapeños. The heat (capsaicin) from the peppers lives in the whiteish membranes and seeds. Add the jalapeños and blitz again two or three times.
    • For a mild salsa, remove all of the seeds and membranes.
    • For spicier salsa, remove the seeds from all the peppers and the membranes from the halved jalapeños.
    • For blazing salsa add the peppers in their entirety or add your favorite fiery ghost pepper or Scotch bonnet.
      • Always start with less of the peppers, you can always turn the heat up but turning it down is significantly more difficult.
      • TIP: Wear gloves when handling the cut jalapeños!
  3. Add the juice of the lemons and limes, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Give the processor another whirl.
  4. Strip down the cilantro and give it a chop. Cilantro isn't everyone's cup of tea and there is a genetic trait that makes it taste like soap to some, so add a little at a time. A tablespoon or so is a good place to start. Add the cilantro and garlic and blitz it again.
    • At this point the bowl should be well blended and finely minced. Depending on the size of your food processor bowl you may need to move the contents to another bowl for this next part.
  5. Quarter the remaining tomatoes and add them to food processor. If you have the room, blend the tomatoes down to your desired size and give it a taste. If you've separated the ingredients, blitz the tomatoes down to roughly your desired size.
  6. Add in the bowl of ingredients and blitz again to combine. Don't worry if there's some left in the side bowl. Give the blend a taste and adjust as needed.
  7. Once you're satisfied add it to the side bowl and give it a mix. Give it another taste, just to make sure.
  8. Cover and refrigerate for at least an hour. The longer it spends melding, the happier it will get.

Notes:

  • Remember to wash all your ingredients before preparing them.
  • This recipe uses a food processor for consistency.
  • The same can be accomplished by dicing all of the vegetables.
