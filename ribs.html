Grilled Ribs

Craving the ultimate barbecue experience? Look no further! Our grilled baby back ribs are a guaranteed crowd-pleaser, perfect for any backyard gathering or family dinner. Get ready to impress your guests with the irresistible aroma and rich, savory taste of these classic baby back ribs.

Ingredients

  • 1 rack of baby back ribs
  • Dry Rub
    • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
    • 1 tablespoon paprika
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon onion powder
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin (optional)
  • Barbecue Sauce Glaze
    • 1 cup barbecue sauce of your choice
    • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar (optional, for sweetness)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ribs
    • Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs if it's still attached. Use a paper towel to grip and pull it off.
    • Rinse the ribs under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Apply the Dry Rub
    • In a small bowl, combine all the dry rub ingredients: brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, chili powder, and cumin if using.
    • Rub the dry rub mixture evenly over both sides of the ribs, pressing gently to adhere.
  3. Prepare the Grill
    • Preheat your grill to medium heat (around 300-325째F or 150-160째C) for indirect cooking. If using charcoal, set up your grill for indirect heat by placing coals on one side.
  4. Grill the Ribs
    • Place the ribs on the grill, meaty side up, away from direct heat.
    • Close the lid and cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on the thickness of the ribs, maintaining the grill temperature around 300째F (150째C).
    • Monitor the temperature and adjust vents as needed to maintain consistent heat.
  5. Apply the Barbecue Sauce Glaze
    • About 30 minutes before the ribs are done, start applying the barbecue sauce glaze. Brush a generous amount of barbecue sauce over the ribs, covering them evenly.
    • Continue cooking for another 15-30 minutes, brushing more sauce on every 10 minutes or so, until the ribs are tender and the sauce is caramelized.
  6. Serve the Ribs
    • Carefully remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing between the bones.
    • Serve hot with extra barbecue sauce on the side, if desired.
