Smoked Hot Wings

Smoked hot wings can be incredibly flavorful and juicy, with a delicious smoky essence. These wings are packed with flavor and perfect for game day or any occasion where you want to enjoy delicious, juicy wings with a smoky twist! Here's how to prepare them.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 lbs chicken wings
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Ranch or blue cheese dressing, for serving (optional)
  • Celery sticks, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken Wings
    • Rinse the chicken wings under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.
    • Trim off any excess skin if desired.
  2. Prepare the Marinade
    • In a bowl, whisk together hot sauce, melted butter, olive oil, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Marinate the Wings
    • Place the chicken wings in a large resealable plastic bag or a bowl.
    • Pour the marinade over the wings and toss until they are evenly coated.
    • Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or overnight for deeper flavor.
  4. Prepare the Smoker
    • Prepare your smoker according to manufacturer’s instructions and preheat it to 225-250°F (107-121°C).
    • Use wood chips or chunks (like hickory, applewood, or mesquite) for smoking.
  5. Smoke the Wings
    • Arrange the marinated wings on the smoker racks, leaving space between each wing for smoke circulation.
    • Smoke the wings for about 2-2.5 hours, or until they reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
    • Flip the wings halfway through the cooking time for even smoking.
  6. Serve the Wings
    • Once smoked, remove the wings from the smoker and let them rest for a few minutes.
    • Serve hot with ranch or blue cheese dressing on the side for dipping, and celery sticks for crunch.

Tips:

  • Adjust Spice Level: Increase or decrease the amount of hot sauce according to your spice preference.
  • Crispiness: If you prefer a crispier texture, you can finish the wings on a hot grill or under a broiler for a few minutes after smoking.
