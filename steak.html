Reverse Seared Steak

Reverse searing is a fantastic method for cooking steak, allowing for a perfectly cooked interior with a flavorful crust. Here's how to prepare a reverse seared steak with a butter baste.

Ingredients

  • 1-2 thick-cut steaks (such as ribeye, striploin, or tenderloin)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil or high smoke point oil (like avocado oil)
  • 2-3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Garlic cloves, for additional flavor (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (thyme or rosemary), for additional flavor (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preparation
    • Preheat your oven to a low temperature, around 250°F (120°C). This low temperature will gently cook the steak through to your desired doneness.
  2. Season the Steak
    • Pat the steaks dry with paper towels to ensure a good sear later.
    • Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
  3. Cooking in the Oven
    • Place the seasoned steaks on a wire rack set over a baking sheet or directly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    • Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the steak if you have one.
    • Roast the steaks in the preheated oven until they reach an internal temperature of about 10-15°F (5-8°C) below your desired doneness. For example, if you want a medium-rare steak, aim for around 115-120°F (46-49°C).
  4. Searing the Steak
    • While the steaks are roasting, prepare a cast iron skillet or heavy-duty frying pan over high heat.
    • Add the vegetable oil and heat until it just starts to smoke.
    • Carefully remove the steaks from the oven and place them directly into the hot skillet.
    • Sear each side for about 1-2 minutes, or until a golden-brown crust forms.
      • If using garlic cloves and herbs, add them to the skillet along with the butter during the searing process.
  5. Butter Baste
    • Once the steaks are seared, reduce the heat to medium and add the butter to the skillet.
    • Tilt the skillet slightly so the butter pools to one side.
    • Use a spoon to continuously baste the steaks with the melted butter, garlic, and herbs for about 1-2 minutes. This adds flavor and helps to develop a rich crust.
  6. Resting and Serving
    • Remove the steaks from the skillet and let them rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring a tender and juicy steak.
    • Slice against the grain and serve immediately.
      • Optionally, drizzle any remaining butter from the skillet over the sliced steak for extra flavor.

Notes:

  • Steak temperatures refer to the level of doneness based on the internal temperature of the meat. Here are the common steak temperatures along with their descriptions:
    1. Rare:
      • Internal Temperature: 120-130°F (49-54°C)
      • Description: Cool red center, very juicy and tender.
    2. Medium Rare:
      • Internal Temperature: 130-135°F (54-57°C)
      • Description: Warm red center, very juicy with a hint of pink.
    3. Medium:
      • Internal Temperature: 135-145°F (57-63°C)
      • Description: Pink center, firm and juicy with a slight reduction in pinkness.
    4. Medium Well:
      • Internal Temperature: 145-155°F (63-68°C)
      • Description: Slightly pink center, mostly gray-brown throughout, less juicy.
    5. Well Done:
      • Internal Temperature: 160°F and above (71°C and above)
      • Description: Uniformly brown throughout, fully cooked with no pink, often drier.

Tips for Checking Doneness:

  • Thermometer: The most accurate way to check doneness is with an instant-read meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the steak.
  • Touch Test: With practice, you can use the touch test to gauge doneness:
    • Rare: Very soft and squishy.
    • Medium Rare: Soft and yielding with some resistance.
    • Medium: Firmer with slight resistance.
    • Well Done: Firm with significant resistance.

Important Notes:

  • The temperatures mentioned are for when you should remove the steak from heat, as it will continue to cook and rise in temperature by a few degrees during resting.
