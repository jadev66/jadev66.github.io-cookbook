Hearty Three Meat Chili

This chili is perfect for a cozy meal, especially on colder days. Enjoy your hearty three-meat chili!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound country-style pork ribs, boneless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 pound ground sausage (like Italian sausage or breakfast sausage)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium jalapeños (seeded and membranes removed), diced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed (optional)
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chili, garlic, and onion powder, cumin, salt and pepper to coat beef/pork
  • Olive oil, for cooking
  • Optional Toppings:
    • Shredded cheese
    • Sour cream
    • Chopped green onions
    • Cilantro

Instructions

  1. Rest the meat ingredients for 20 minutes on the counter. Then sprinkle the beef and ribs with chili powder, onion powder, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to coat. Allow to rest another 20 minutes.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a bit of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef, country-style pork ribs, and ground sausage. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until browned and cooked through.
  3. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and bell pepper to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes (with juices), tomato sauce, kidney beans (if using), and beef broth or water.
  5. Add the chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  6. Bring the chili to a simmer. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for at least 1 hour (longer if possible), stirring occasionally.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper, cumin, and chili powder if needed.
  8. Serve hot, topped with your favorite toppings such as shredded cheese, sour cream, chopped green onions, or cilantro.

Tips:

  • Once the meat is cooked, remove the meat to a separate bowl and deglaze the bottom with beer or lager. Bring the beer to a boil and reduce by half. Add the meat with juices in and continue from Step 2.
  • To add some heat, add a half teaspoon of dried cayenne pepper. You can also add spiciness by leaving one or both jalapeños in tact and sliced into rings.
  • For a richer flavor substitute bone broth for beef broth.
